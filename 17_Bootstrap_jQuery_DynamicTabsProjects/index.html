Software Development Projects (SDP)

SDPs at VIT involve real-world software development, emphasizing problem-solving, coding, and teamwork. Students gain hands-on experience with software design, implementation, testing, and maintenance.

Engineering Design & Innovation (EDI)

Course Codes: CS2242 and CS3060
Credits: 4
Course Prerequisites: Problem Based Learning

Course Objectives:
- Develop critical thinking and problem-solving ability by exploring and proposing solutions to realistic/social problems.
- Evaluate alternative approaches and justify the use of selected tools and methods.
- Emphasize learning activities that are long-term, interdisciplinary, and student-centric.
- Engage students in rich and authentic learning experiences.
- Provide every student the opportunity to get involved individually or as a group to develop team skills and learn professionalism.
- Develop an ecosystem to promote entrepreneurship and research culture among students.

Design Thinking (DT)

Scope: Applicable for S.Y. B.Tech. and T.Y. B.Tech. students as a group activity.
Objective: To provide an ecosystem for students to publish papers and file patents.
Outcome: Publication of paper or filing of patent.
Assessment and Grading:
Grades based on publication or patent filing status as outlined in the grading table.

Course Projects

Scope: Course projects are mandatory for every subject and are aimed at enhancing the understanding and application of the concepts learned during the course.
Objective: To enable students to apply theoretical knowledge to solve real-world problems, enhancing their learning experience and preparing them for professional challenges.
Outcome: The successful completion of course projects results in a deeper understanding of the subject matter, improved problem-solving skills, and practical experience that is valuable in the professional world.
